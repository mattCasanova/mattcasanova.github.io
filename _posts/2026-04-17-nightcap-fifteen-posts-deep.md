It's 11:10 on a Friday. I have a drink. I've got no business writing another post tonight — I already shipped one this morning — and yet here we are.

Quick accounting. I started this blog on Monday. It is now Friday. In that window I have, depending on how you count, published fifteen posts. A GPU terminal origin story. Three on AI and taste. [Four posts of bad advice](/series/bad-advice/). Two of [house rules](/series/house-rules/). A Vegas story about a guy whose name I don't remember. And a handful of Flux session writeups that are basically public diary entries with syntax highlighting.

Not sharing it yet. Nobody's reading this. Probably nobody for a while. That's fine. This week wasn't about an audience — it was about seeing whether I could actually do this. Sit down every night. Throw the ideas at the wall.

Turns out I can. Turns out I kind of can't stop.

### The Ledger

**Blog.** Bad Advice is done, four deep. House Rules is two deep — S and O are out, L is giving me trouble. I've been through multiple drafts of the Liskov post because I can't find the narrative. Some nights Claude is great at story structure. Some nights it's not. Those are the nights the post just sits there.

**Flux.** Decent progress. Less than I wanted. I had to [backtrack on the roadmap](/2026/04/13/vim-can-wait/) mid-week — I was polishing Vim rendering before the basics (paste, autocomplete, history) even worked. A terminal you can't type in is not a terminal. New roadmap is in. v0.2 is the daily-driver milestone. Vim polish is in v1.x where it belongs. Also: Rust is close enough to C++ that I can read it, far enough that every review doubles as a language lesson. Sessions take longer. That's the cost of the combo platter — new language *and* a project type I've never built before.

**LiquidMetal2D.** The OCP post caught a real bug. I was writing about the `toUniform()` coupling, went to double-check the code, and found I'd only gone halfway on it back in 2020. Fixed it while writing the post. That's a new thing — post pushes the engine forward instead of just describing it. Still want particles. Particles can wait; I've been saying that for five years, they'll keep.[^1]

**Other side projects.** The iOS app and the web app behind it are making good progress. More to say later.

### One more thing

Claude Opus 4.7 just dropped. This post is the first thing I'm writing in a 4.7 session. No strong take yet — and if this one reads funny, now you know why.[^2]

Also: I'm headed to Vegas tomorrow morning.

Vegas, baby.

I'll probably blog from there too, because apparently that's who I am now.

Alright. It's past 11. I'm not cracking Liskov open tonight. Tonight is for the drink.

See you, space cowboy.
