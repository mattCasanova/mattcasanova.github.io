I'm **Matt Casanova**. This is my night-shift blog.

I'm a software engineer at Meta. Before that, I taught C/C++ and game development as a college lecturer for seven years and [co-authored a book](https://www.amazon.com/Game-Development-Patterns-Best-Practices-ebook/dp/B01MRP7SPA/) on game development patterns. I've been programming for about 20 years across more languages than I care to count. I've been building mobile apps since my first iOS game, [Sync-Ball](https://apps.apple.com/ca/app/sync-ball/id356277312), shipped in 2010, back when the App Store was two years old and Android Studio didn't exist yet.

My day job is mobile and web. My nights are game engines and terminals.

## What I'm Building

**[Flux](https://github.com/mattcasanova/flux)** — an open-source, GPU-accelerated terminal emulator written in Rust. Warp-style command blocks. Zero telemetry. No account required. Built on top of the excellent work of [Alacritty](https://github.com/alacritty/alacritty), [WezTerm's `portable-pty`](https://crates.io/crates/portable-pty), [cosmic-text](https://github.com/pop-os/cosmic-text), and [wgpu](https://github.com/gfx-rs/wgpu). Currently in Phase 1 — the kind of phase where Vim almost looks right.

**[LiquidMetal2D](https://github.com/mattCasanova/LiquidMetal2D)** — an open-source 2D game engine I built in Swift + Metal back in 2020 and recently revived. Instanced rendering, a component system, a spatial grid broadphase that runs 7,000 colliding ships at 30fps on a phone. I picked it back up this year because I wanted to, and because I can move faster on it now than I could have on my own five years ago.

**[Tokyo Night Storm iTerm2 profile](https://github.com/mattCasanova/tokyo-night-storm-iterm-profile)** — I use Tokyo Night Storm everywhere (VS Code, PHPStorm, Android Studio, Xcode), and when I couldn't find a good iTerm2 version of it, I built one and shipped it. I love tweaking themes. My dotfiles are a whole thing.

## What This Blog Is

Notes from the night shift. Things I'm thinking about while I work on these projects, usually late, usually with a drink nearby. Topics:

- **Rust, GPU rendering, and terminal internals** — as I figure out how to build Flux
- **Game engine patterns** — because it turns out a terminal is mostly a sprite renderer, and I've been writing sprite renderers for 20 years
- **AI-assisted development** — how I actually use Claude Code to move faster on side projects, the collaboration patterns that work, the failure modes that don't
- **Software engineering philosophy** — the stuff you learn after a decade or two of shipping, which is mostly that syntax is the least of our skills and the hardest part is knowing which old pattern fits the new problem

If a post ends with *"time for a drink,"* that's just how the night shift goes.
